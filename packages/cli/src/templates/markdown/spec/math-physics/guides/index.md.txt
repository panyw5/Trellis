# Research Thinking Guides

> **Purpose**: expand your reasoning before turning mathematical structure into code, experiments, or claims.

---

## Available Guides

| Guide | Purpose | When to Use |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| [Abstraction to Computation](./abstraction-to-computation.md) | Turn a definition into a canonical computational object | When introducing a new mathematical structure |
| [Conjecture Validation](./conjecture-validation.md) | Stress-test experimental patterns before trusting them | When a computation suggests a theorem or formula |
| [Reuse Existing Constructions](./reuse-existing-constructions.md) | Avoid duplicating equivalent constructions or benchmark families | When adding a new representation or helper routine |

---

## Core Principle

Research mistakes often come from unclear notation, untested benchmark cases, or treating numerical evidence as proof.
These guides are here to slow you down at the right moments.
